Charred Eggplant

Servings: 4 Source: Simply Japanese

Ingredients

2 eggplant

20 g (¾ oz) fresh ginger

5 shiso leaves (optional)

4 generous pinches katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes) (optional)

Soy sauce

Directions

Wash the eggplant. Poke a few holes in the eggplant with a skewer. Peel and grate the ginger. Cut the shiso leaves into thin strips, if using.

Preheat the oven to 250°C (500°F).

Place the eggplant on a sheet pan and put them in the oven to roast for 10 minutes on each side. Using a skewer, check if they are cooked. The eggplant must be tender right to the center and the skin charred and black.

Remove the eggplant and immediately put them in a paper bag on a plate (the steam will soften the skin). Leave to stand for 10 minutes, then remove the skin by hand.

Cut the eggplant in half and arrange on four plates.

Sprinkle with the grated ginger and, if desired, the shiso leaves and katsuobushi.

Drizzle with soy sauce just before serving.
