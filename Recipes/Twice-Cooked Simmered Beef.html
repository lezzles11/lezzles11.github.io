Twice-Cooked Simmered Beef

Difficulty: Easy Source: app.ckbk.com

Ingredients

750 g/1 lb 10 oz piece stewing beef, most of the fat trimmed off, but not all

4 thick slices peeled fresh root ginger

1 sherry glass of rice wine, sake or dry sherry

50 ml /2 fl oz dark soy sauce

1 heaped tablespoon soft brown sugar

vegetable oil, for frying

sesame oil, to finish

Directions

Put the beef, ginger and rice wine, sake or sherry into a large pan, add just enough water to cover the beef and bring to the boil. Reduce the heat to a simmer, skim off any fat that has floated to the surface, cover the pan and simmer gently for 40-45 minutes.

Remove the beef from the pan and leave to go cold. Reserve the cooking liquid. When the beef is cold, cut it into thin slices. Reheat the broth.

Heat a wok or frying pan, add a little vegetable oil and stir-fry the beef for about 1 minute, then add the soy sauce, sugar and the reserved broth. Cover the wok or pan and simmer for 10 minutes, or until the beef is tender and warmed through.

Pour into a serving dish and sprinkle (I refuse to use the word drizzle!) a few drops of sesame oil to float on top of the dish.
