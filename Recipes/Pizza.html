Pizza

italian

Source: Leo

Ingredients

1,5kg farina / flour

900g acqua / water

37g sale / salt

40g lievito / yeast (if using dry yeast, use one-third of the amount, which would be approximately 13 g)


Directions

1. Prepare the Yeast Mixture:
- If you’re using fresh yeast, crumble it into a small amount of the lukewarm water (taken from the 900 g) in a bowl. Stir until the yeast is dissolved.
- For dry yeast, mix it with a small amount of warm water and a pinch of sugar, and let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients:
- In a large mixing bowl or on a clean countertop, pour out the flour and form a mound.
- Make a well in the center of the mound.

3. Combine the Ingredients:
- Pour the yeast mixture into the well, then start adding the rest of the water gradually.
- Begin mixing the flour into the liquid from the center outwards, slowly bringing in more flour to the mix.
- As the dough starts to form, sprinkle the salt over the dough and continue to mix.

4. Knead the Dough:
- Once the ingredients are roughly combined, transfer the dough to a clean, floured surface.
- Knead the dough for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, you can add a little bit more flour, but do so sparingly.

5. First Rise (Bulk Fermentation):
- Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a large bowl that has been lightly floured or oiled to prevent sticking.
- Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place until it doubles in size, which may take about 1-2 hours depending on the room temperature and the potency of the yeast.

Shape the Pizza:
- Preheat your oven to its highest setting, ideally 250-300°C (475-575°F), and if you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up.
- Take one portion of the dough and on a floured surface, press it out from the center, working your way outwards to form a flat, round pizza shape with a slightly thicker edge.
- You can use a rolling pin or stretch it with your hands to the desired thickness.

Add Toppings and Bake:
- Once shaped, add your desired pizza sauce, cheese, and other toppings.
- Transfer the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or a baking sheet.
- Bake in the oven for about 5-10 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.

Serve:
- Carefully remove the pizza from the oven, slice, and serve hot!

Notes

The crust needs to be quite thin, roll it out well
Can use half the recipe for a whole pizza
